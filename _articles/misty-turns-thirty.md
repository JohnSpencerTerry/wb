This past weekend my dad and I flew down to Atlanta, rented a car, and drove out to our hometown of Cartersville, Georgia for a few days. We went to see our horse Misty, who turned thirty.

Thirty is a big number for a horse, so it was a good excuse to make the trip.

<figure class="media-figure">
  <img src="/assets/photos/misty.jpg" alt="Misty." />
</figure>

Misty lived at our home in Cartersville for most of her life. A few years ago, after my sister and I moved to New York, my parents decided to leave Georgia and head to Essex, Connecticut — partly to be closer to family, where they're both originally from, and partly to be a shorter drive from us. My parents sold the property, and Misty stayed in Georgia — she's now boarded at Blackberry Pastures.

The farm threw Misty a birthday party.
