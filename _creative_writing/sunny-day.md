## Outline

**Opening.** Her kitchen, Saturday morning, February. Kids in pajamas at the counter. The radio on low.

**Beat 1 — morning at home, the world woven in.** She moves through the morning routine while the radio plays. Worldbuilding interspersed through fragments she half-hears: the Iowa Farmers Union renewed its three-year contract, the rates holding; a history segment about the early days — the Dubai pilot, the Phoenix proposal, what the engineers had first envisioned it for (cities running out of water). The kids ask for more cereal. She refills the bowl. Packs the work bag. Kisses them. Tells her husband she'll be back by five.

**Beat 2 — drive in.** Snow on the road. Heater going. She turns down the long driveway and the snow stops at the hedge in a clean line. She's seen it before, not often. She parks inside the bubble. Air warm the moment she opens the door.

**Beat 3 — the prep.** Inside the house. Thermostat, water, the small tasks she always does to open a Saturday. The bubble hums faintly through the screens, a sound you stop hearing after a while.

**Beat 4 — the find-out and the decision.** Mid-prep, the text comes in. Owners not coming. Weather already paid for through Monday. She stands with the phone for a moment longer than she needs to. Then she texts her husband. Brief, plain. Bring the kids after lunch. His reply takes a minute. Then: ok.

**Beat 5 — the prep, redirected.** She keeps working but the work has shifted underneath her. Pool cover off. Towels laid out on the chaise. A tray of fruit from the fridge — not theirs, hers now, sort of.

**Beat 6 — arrival.** Early afternoon. Her husband pulls up, kids in the back. They cross the property line and shed their coats onto the gravel before they've taken three steps. He doesn't get out. A small wave through the windshield and he's gone.

**Beat 7 — the property line and the older kid.** Mid-afternoon, she stands at the edge of the lawn near the hedge. Snow on the neighbor's roof on the other side. A cardinal on a branch in the cold. The older one comes up and finds her there. Doesn't ask the obvious question. Says something smaller. She answers in kind. The younger one is yelling for them to come back in.

**Closing.** Late afternoon. She's on the deck with a glass of water. The kids are in the pool, the older one floating, the younger one diving for something at the bottom. The sun lower now but still warm. The bubble holds. She watches them have it.
